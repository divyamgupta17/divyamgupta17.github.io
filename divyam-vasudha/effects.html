Global Warming

The Earth's average surface temperature has increased significantly, leading to more frequent and intense heat waves.

Extreme Weather Events

There has been an increase in the frequency and severity of extreme weather events, including hurricanes, droughts, and floods.

Sea-Level Rise

Melting glaciers and ice caps, along with the thermal expansion of seawater, have contributed to rising sea levels, threatening coastal communities and ecosystems.

Ocean Acidification

Increased CO2 absorption by oceans has led to acidification, impacting marine life and coral reefs.

Biodiversity Loss

Many species are struggling to adapt to the rapid changes in their environments, leading to shifts in ecosystems and increased extinction rates.

Agricultural Impacts

Changes in temperature, precipitation patterns, and extreme weather events affect agricultural productivity and food security. Crop yields are threatened by droughts, floods, heat stress, and shifts in pest and disease patterns. Smallholder farmers, especially in developing countries, face increased vulnerability to climate variability.

Health Risks

Climate change poses direct and indirect risks to human health. Heatwaves and extreme temperatures increase the incidence of heat-related illnesses and deaths. Changes in precipitation patterns influence water availability and quality, affecting sanitation and the spread of waterborne diseases. Additionally, vector-borne diseases like malaria and dengue fever are expanding into new regions as temperatures rise.
